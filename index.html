Brian Seonggeun Park

I am an undergraduate student studying Computer Science and Engineering at UCLA where I am a member of the Structures-Computer Interaction (SCI) Lab. My advisors are Professors M. Khalid Jawed and Jungseock Joo.

My research focus involves robotics, computer vision, and deep learning. More specifically, I have primarily worked in 3D Reconstruction from 2D images, as well as robot perception algorithms for detection of Deformable Linear Objects.

Source Code & Data / Video

Robust and rapid instance segmentation of DLOs via skeleton pixel traversals. Segmentations are generated on topologically corrected skeleton structures with intersections handled by the simple and physically insightful optimization objective of minimizing cumulative bending energy.

Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU)
Fall 2022

In the fall, I worked on two different projects:
For Simulation Framework for Development of Robots and IoT for Precision Agriculture, I created several different NeRFs of different plant species using NVIDIA's Instant-NGP.
For Realtime Deformable Linear ObjectDetection through Minimal BendingEnergy Superpixel Traversals, I compiled a dataset of 300 images and created ground truth labels for them using NumPy and OpenCV.

Winter 2023

In the fall, I primarily worked on Simulation Framework for Development of Robots and IoT for Precision Agriculture. I implemented an data sampling program in Unity that captures images of 3D objects from various view angles.

Spring 2023

I continued implementing the Unity Sampler this quarter, for Simulation Framework for Development of Robots and IoT for Precision Agriculture. I wrote a Python script that compiled a JSON file, in order to bypass the use of COLMAP in creating the NeRF. I also wrote a bash script that builds and trains a NeRF, and then outputs a mesh file of that NeRF.
